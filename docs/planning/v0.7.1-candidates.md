# v0.7.1 candidates — deferred findings from the final v0.7.0 six-lens review (2026-07-18)

Findings from the pre-release six-agent review (release machinery / v18 wire / server
pipeline / client state machine / test-gate integrity / docs) that were validated as REAL
but deliberately deferred — each was judged not release-blocking (experimental-path-only,
rare-corner, tooling-only, or nuisance-severity) and riskier to change pre-tag than to ship.
What WAS fixed pre-tag: the client byte-dimension backpressure halt, `DeferredTicketReleases`
per-entry containment, literal wire-identity byte pins (both platforms), the v18 serve-source
decode-value pin, the recursive gametest entrypoint scan, `release_check.py` nested-common /
per-family presence / `discover()` selftest coverage, the A5 comment direction, and the doc
corrections. See the review round in git history (feat/want-set-requests, 2026-07-18).

## Production

1. **Folia hold-release CAS can resurrect a superseded batch** (MEDIUM; Folia experimental —
   no 26.2 Folia exists upstream to even run it). `republishHeldBatch`'s
   `compareAndSet(null, held)` guards "mailbox empty now", not "no offer since the hold": if a
   newer batch B passes THROUGH the mailbox (router takes it) during the one-tick hold of A,
   the republish succeeds and stale A replaces B's backlog. Self-heals in ≤1 s via
   re-declaration EXCEPT when B was the edge-triggered empty backpressure clear (client goes
   silent → resurrected A refills the backlog of a client that declared it cannot ingest).
   Fix shape preserving the pin: an offer-generation counter on `AbstractPlayerRequestState`
   (bump on every `offerIncomingBatch`; pump records it at hold time; republish only if
   unchanged). `PaperRequestProcessingService.holdAndScheduleRegionProbe` +
   `AbstractPlayerRequestState.republishHeldBatch`.
2. **`OffThreadProcessor.shutdown()` interrupt can race the shutdown sentinel** (LOW): the
   post-sentinel `interrupt()` can hit the JLS notify-vs-interrupt window (or, deterministic,
   the 10-error backoff sleep) and skip the sentinel branch that applies queued timestamp
   invalidations before the final cache save → stale `up_to_date` across restart until the
   chunk is re-edited. Fix shape: consume a pending sentinel inside the InterruptedException
   handler, or join once before interrupting. (`OffThreadProcessor` ~:330-356, :938-964.)
3. **Paper/client containment logs unthrottled** (LOW, log-spam vector): Paper's malformed-C2S
   warn/error sites (`PaperPayloadHandler` decode-null warn, `LSSPaperPlugin.dispatchPluginMessage`
   catch) and the client's unknown-batch-response-type warn (`LSSClientNetworking` dispatch)
   log once per hostile frame/entry with no throttle. Route through a rate limiter (the
   processor's `DROP_WARN_INTERVAL_MS` pattern).
4. **Folia shutdown-window WARN storm** (LOW): `PaperChunkGenerationService.completeAsyncLoad`'s
   schedule-failure path lacks the warn-once latch its null-chunk siblings have.
5. **Folia runtime plugin-disable race** (LOW): a region-thread `onChunkReady` can race
   `PaperChunkGenerationService.shutdown()`'s plain-collection clears (CME on a dying plugin).
6. **Client render-distance-shrink annulus** (LOW): rings that fall out of the vanilla view
   circle after a stationary render-distance reduction sit below `confirmedRing` and are not
   re-walked until movement/dirty/reconnect. Detect `viewDistance` shrink → `resetConfirmedRing`.
7. **`ColumnCacheStore.MAX_CACHE_ENTRIES` (2M) vs `MAX_LOD_DISTANCE` (2048)** (LOW corner):
   an effective distance > ~675 chunks can legitimately save more entries than load accepts →
   whole-file discard each session. Align the caps or make save enforce the load bound.
8. **`ClientSessionGate.onJoin` nulls a live manager without teardown** (LOW, precondition
   unverified): if Fabric ever fires JOIN without a preceding DISCONNECT, the old session's
   cache save is skipped. One-line hardening: teardown if non-null on JOIN.
9. **A5 boundary accounting** (diagnosis aid): the two generation-ticket drop paths in
   `drainGenerationTicketRequests` (state gone / dimension mismatch, both platforms) skip
   `miss_dropped` — now documented in CLAUDE.md's flake catalog + `law_A5`'s comment; a
   counter at those sites would close the identity properly. Since the miss memo, a
   memo-hit-admitted ticket dropped there also leaves its `disk.memo_hits` +1 unbalanced
   (same window/magnitude). Related latent hole for a future MULTI-client soak: a dedup
   fan-out miss counts `disk.not_found` once but each REFUSED attachee (pacing/spread/
   slot-full) adds its own `miss_dropped` — over-counts the RHS by one per refused
   attachee (documented in `law_A5`'s comment, 2026-07-19).
10. **Dimension-keyed maps never pruned for unloaded datapack dimensions** (LOW):
    `DirtyContentFilter.hashesByDimension` + `DirtyColumnTracker` retain unloaded-dimension
    state for the server's life.

10a. **Server-side negative "not-generated" memo — full implementation plan now at
   `docs/planning/miss-memo-design.md`** (2026-07-19; supersedes this sketch — the plan
   adds the generation-finish clear matrix, the already-generated fall-through pins, the
   A5 `memo_hits` term, and the per-chunk-over-region decision). Original sketch:
   Under server-owned generation, a position waiting for a gen slot re-reads disk at ~1 Hz
   (the deliberate miss-churn loop pinned by generation-capacity-stress). A short-TTL
   server-side memo of "disk miss observed at T" consulted at the disk-admission rung
   (AFTER the up-to-date + loaded-probe rungs) would skip the redundant read and fall
   through to `handleDiskNotFound` directly. Constraints that make it safe — all mandatory:
   - **TTL-bounded (~5-10 s), never invalidation-only** — the dirty/save hooks are known
     incomplete (Paper walk-in gen unmarked by default; C2ME may bypass `ChunkMap.save`),
     and today's churn IS the healing for those gaps. Staleness must cost seconds, not a
     session. The probe rung staying ahead of the memo closes the loaded-chunk case free.
   - **Law A5 gains a term** — a memo-hit gen submission has no `disk.not_found`; a new
     counter (e.g. `disk.memo_hits`) must enter the identity + checker + selftests.
   - **Separate map, no timestamp-sentinel overloading** (`packedXZ → expiry`) — the 0-stamp
     ghost history forbids reusing the cache's value space.
   - Cleared on: any serve/stamp of the position, dirty mark, generation delivery, TTL.
   - **Consider the region-granular variant first** ("region file absent", 32×32) — kills the
     fresh-world bulk with ~1000× fewer entries and a far smaller staleness surface.
   Gate on evidence: profile fresh-backfill on realistic (non-SSD/network) storage before
   building — every current harness regime is green without it.

## CI / tooling

11. **release.yml has no Tier-3 guard**: a docs-only release commit gets no build.yml run, and
    release.yml never runs Tier 3 — a tag can ship without Tier 3 having run on the release
    commit (human checklist is the only gate). Candidate: a pre-publish step querying the
    Build workflow's conclusion for `$GITHUB_SHA` (needs `actions: read`).
12. **release.yml Tier 2 has no retry-once** (build.yml's flake containment) — pre-publish
    red, friction only.
13. **release_check glob validation mirrors, doesn't parse, the six workflow glob sites** —
    a future rename can desync a `mc-publish files:` glob and fail mid-publish.
14. **check_soak selftest gaps**: the traffic-floor anti-vacuity guard, the server end-row
    completion gate, and the pass-only arms in several named checks (generation-disabled
    floors, bandwidth-throttle guards, storm generation floor) have no catch fixtures; A5 is
    single-client-sound only (dedup fan-out adds `miss_dropped` with no left-side term if a
    multi-client soak is ever added); `EXPECTED_RUNS` membership unvalidated.
15. **Entrypoint contract residuals**: nested/second top-level classes carrying `@GameTest`
    inside a listed file are invisible; FQ annotation form unmatched.
16. ~~**VSS cosmetics**: Paper VSS `website:` still points at the LSS Modrinth page; the Sodium
    config-screen title is hardcoded "LOD Server Support" in both jars.~~ **DONE pre-v0.7.0
    (2026-07-19)**: the Paper voxyJar rewrites `website:` (pair check requires both rewritten
    lines to differ), and the Sodium config screen derives title + icon from the jar's own
    fabric.mod.json. Deliberate non-changes: the Paper console enable/disable lines and the
    in-jar LICENSE entry name state the shared LodServerSupport identity on both brandings.

17. **v16 shim server-glue seams untested on both platforms**: `handleBatchRequest`'s v16
    branch (merge routing, byte-0 bounce frame, rangeFiltered), the column egress seam
    (`isV16 → asV16()/rewriteColumnToV16 → send → onColumnSent prune` + warn-once drop),
    `drainSendActions → observeBatchResponse` wiring, and the `tickV16Compat` ordering pin

18. **Fabric dirty detection unverified under chunk-IO-overhaul mods** — added to the manual
    C2ME smoke list in CLAUDE.md (the `ChunkMap.save` hook could be bypassed or moved
    off-thread by a chunkio rewrite).
19. **Miss-memo deferred pins (from the 2026-07-19 miss-memo review round)** — the design
    doc's plan promised these and the branch shipped without them (behavior is live-soak
    covered; these are the durable pins): (a) the requirement-2 "already generated by
    another process" fall-through — Fabric Tier-2 gametest (write a region chunk, seed a
    memo, declare, assert load-served with correct content, no NOT_GENERATED) and the
    Paper `launchAsyncLoad`-seam twin; (b) the Tier-2 memo lifecycle gametest (miss →
    memo-skip re-read → generation → served → memo cleared); (c) literal Paper twins for
    the memo/pacing common tests (currently Fabric-side only, common code); (d) clear-
    matrix all-air + dimension-change flavors; (e) a `claimsData` propagation pin through
    the memo rung (ts>0 → escalated PendingRequest claims data — a hard-coded false would
    answer up_to_date instead of the clearing column for a resync client on an all-air
    generation outcome).
20. **Miss-memo map budget accounting** (LOW, memory-only — production item, filed here to
    keep the miss-memo pair together): miss maps evict against their
    own per-dimension cap instead of sharing the stamp budget (worst case ~1.5x the
    modeled memory at cap, ~16 MB/dimension at the 32 MB default), and expired entries
    persist until a read touches them or wholesale eviction — a long exploration session
    ratchets toward cap with dead entries. Options: count both maps against one budget;
    opportunistic expiry sweep on `evictIfOversized`. Bounded + kill-switchable today.
21. **Config kill switch for generation pacing / frontier damping** (release-sweep flag,
    2026-07-19): `MAX_GENERATION_COHORT_SPAN` and `FRONTIER_OUTWARD_DAMP_MILLIS_PER_RING`
    are constants — `missMemoTtlSeconds: 0` disables only the memo, and ttl=0 no longer
    restores pre-memo ordering (pacing is ttl-independent). Shipped as constants
    deliberately: every refusal is the standard safe transient drop (worst case = slower
    far fill, never data loss or NOT_GENERATED), per-player, observable via
    `gen_order_gated` + `[lss-adm]`. If a field report ever needs a live lever, add one
    config knob (0 = pacing off) rather than exposing the individual constants.
22. **Voxy view-distance cache refreshes per CALL, not per tick** (LOW, pre-existing since
    v0.2.0 — final-review finding 2026-07-19): `SpiralScanner.getCachedVoxyDistance()`
    (`fabric/.../client/SpiralScanner.java:248`) bumps `voxyDistanceStaleness` on every
    invocation, but the field comment assumes a 20 Hz tick driver that does not exist.
    Real drivers: the 1 Hz scan (`:98`), each chunk crossing and **each received column**
    (`LodRequestManager.java:172/338`), plus diag/soak reads. Consequences in both
    directions: stationary + converged, a Voxy render-distance change is noticed after
    ~20 s instead of ~1 s (compounds candidate #6's shrink annulus); during heavy backfill
    the reflective `ModCompat.getVoxyViewDistanceChunks()` probe fires ~10-25x/s on the
    client thread — inverting the caching intent exactly when the client is busiest. Fix
    shape: drive the counter from the scanner's existing tick increment and make
    `getCachedVoxyDistance()` a pure read.
23. **Want-set budget scaling is count-only while the backpressure halt is count-OR-bytes**
    (LOW, final-review finding 2026-07-19): the graduated backoff feeding the scanner
    (`SpiralScanner.java:72-74`) scales by `1 - columnQueueSize/haltThreshold` only, but
    `haltedByBackpressure` (`LodRequestManager.java:190`) fires on either the count or the
    byte cap. On a server serving large columns (tall/modded worlds) the byte budget binds
    first, so the client declares the full 800-position want-set at full rate right up to
    the byte-halt edge and then hard-stops with the edge-triggered empty clear — the
    designed soft-landing is absent in the byte dimension. CONTAINED by design (the halt
    still fires ahead of the drop regime, so no ingest-failure strikes are burned). Fix
    shape: take the min of the two pressure ratios, threading `columnQueueBytes` /
    `byteHaltThreshold()` through `maybeScan`.
